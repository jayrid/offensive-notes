## Executive Summary
The OWASP Juice Shop application was subjected to a targeted attack exploiting Insecure Direct Object Reference (IDOR) vulnerabilities. The attacker successfully enumerated 22 users, gained unauthorized administrative access, and exfiltrated sensitive basket data from multiple accounts, including the admin account. This incident resulted in a full compromise of customer purchase data and unauthorized modification of cart items.

## Attack Narrative
The attack proceeded in several distinct phases on 2026-03-12:
- **Reconnaissance (00:01Z):** The attacker identified the application version and performed massive user enumeration using the `/api/Users` endpoint, identifying 22 valid user accounts. Administrative credentials (admin@juice-sh.op) were also harvested during this phase.
- **Scanning (00:06Z):** A focused web layer scan confirmed the attack surface was limited to port 3000/tcp, noting a complete lack of modern security headers (CSP, HSTS) and a wildcard CORS policy.
- **Enumeration (00:09Z):** The attacker confirmed IDOR vulnerabilities on `/rest/basket/:id` by using a low-privilege JWT (User 24) to successfully access baskets belonging to other users.
- **Exploitation (00:13Z):** Three primary exploits were executed: reading the administrative basket (ID 1), reading a customer basket (ID 3), and unauthorized modification of items in Jim's basket (ID 2).
- **Post-Exploitation (00:19Z):** The attacker dumped 6 user baskets and identified further information leaks on the `/rest/track-order/:id` endpoint.

## Attacker Capability Assessment
The attacker demonstrated **intermediate** capability. They efficiently mapped the API structure, identified logical vulnerabilities (IDOR), and scripted the exfiltration of data. The use of basic tools like `curl` and `python3` suggests a methodical approach rather than highly advanced exploit development.

## Defensive Gaps Identified
1. **Lack of Ownership Validation**: The primary failure was the absence of server-side checks to ensure a user owns the resource (basket) they are accessing.
2. **Verbose API Endpoints**: `/api/Users` and `/api/BasketItems` provided excessive information to unauthenticated or low-privilege users.
3. **Missing Security Headers**: The absence of CSP and HSTS increases the risk of further attacks (XSS, MITM).
4. **Permissive CORS**: The wildcard CORS policy allows for potential cross-origin exploitation.
